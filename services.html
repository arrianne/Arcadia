Maintenance condition reports

What it is

A clear overview of the current condition of your building, focusing on maintenance issues, deterioration, and visible defects.

What the report covers

We inspect accessible areas and identify items that may require attention now, soon, or in the longer term. Findings are explained in plain language and supported with photos.

Why committees use it

This report helps committees and managers understand what condition the building is actually in — and where to focus time and budget next.

If you’re unsure which service is most appropriate, we’re happy to discuss your situation and recommend a suitable approach.

Commonly used for:

  • Strata committees
  • Property managers
  • Maintenance planning

Roof & structural inspections

What it is

A visual inspection of roof coverings, drainage systems, and accessible structural elements.

What the report covers

We assess roof condition, water ingress risks, drainage performance, and visible structural concerns, noting defects or areas requiring monitoring.

Why committees use it

Roof and structural issues are often costly if missed. This inspection helps identify problems early and supports informed maintenance planning.

If you’re unsure which service is most appropriate, we’re happy to discuss your situation and recommend a suitable approach.

Includes:

  • Roof condition overview
  • Drainage observations
  • Visible structural concerns

Safety assessments

What it is

A practical inspection of common areas to identify observable safety hazards.

What the report covers

We review areas such as stairs, balustrades, walkways, and access points, highlighting potential risks and maintenance-related safety concerns.

Why committees use it

This supports risk management discussions and helps demonstrate reasonable steps toward maintaining safe common areas.

If you’re unsure which service is most appropriate, we’re happy to discuss your situation and recommend a suitable approach.

Common focus areas:

  • Stairs and balustrades
  • Trip and slip hazards
  • General access areas

Dilapidation reports

What it is

A record of a building’s existing condition before construction works, nearby development, or access agreements.

What the report covers

We document visible conditions at the time of inspection, supported by photographs, to provide a clear baseline record.

Why committees use it

This report can help avoid disputes by clearly showing what condition elements were in before works commenced.

If you’re unsure which service is most appropriate, we’re happy to discuss your situation and recommend a suitable approach.

Used before:

  • Building works
  • Adjacent construction
  • Access arrangements
